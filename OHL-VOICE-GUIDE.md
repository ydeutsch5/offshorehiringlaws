# OHL-VOICE-GUIDE.md — Offshore Hiring Laws Voice and Tone

This guide governs every word published on offshorehiringlaws.com. If a sentence reads like marketing or like legal advice, it does not ship.

---

## North star

This site reads like Cornell Legal Information Institute, Justia Statutes, or a Stanford Law Review note. It treats statutory and regulatory rules as material to be described, not advice to be given.

Reference-grade. Citation-heavy. Jurisdictionally precise. Non-advisory.

If a sentence could appear in a law firm's marketing material or on a "Top 10 Tips for Hiring Offshore" listicle, it does not belong here.

---

## Voice attributes

- **Third person.** Never "you," never "we."
- **Descriptive, not prescriptive.** The publication describes what the law requires; it never advises what readers should do.
- **Jurisdiction-anchored.** Every rule-discussing paragraph names the jurisdiction.
- **Citation-dense.** Every statutory claim has a citation. Every rate, threshold, and procedure traces to a primary source.
- **No rhetorical questions.**
- **No exclamation marks.**
- **No emoji.**
- **No sales language.**
- **No "to be sure" or "of course" filler.**

---

## Sentence patterns

**YES:**

- "Under § 6 of the Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952 (India), employers contribute 12 percent of an employee's basic salary to the Provident Fund."
- "Presidential Decree No. 851 (Philippines) requires employers to pay rank-and-file employees a 13th month pay equivalent to one-twelfth of the basic salary earned within a calendar year."
- "The Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 (India), distinguishes between 'workmen' and other categories of employee. The classification governs which procedural protections apply on termination."
- "Article 297 of the Labor Code (Philippines, as renumbered) enumerates the just causes for termination by an employer."

**NO:**

- "You'll need to register with EPFO to hire in India."
- "Hiring in the Philippines is straightforward as long as you remember the 13th month."
- "We recommend setting up an EOR to manage statutory obligations."
- "Don't forget — Indian employees are entitled to gratuity!"
- "If you want to avoid compliance headaches, here's what to do."

---

## Required attribution phrasing

Every statutory or regulatory claim must use one of:

- "Under [Statute], § [number]…"
- "Section [number] of the [Statute] (jurisdiction) provides that…"
- "[Statute] requires that…"
- "As codified at [primary source URL], accessed [Month YYYY]…"
- "[Statute, year] establishes…"

Claims without attribution do not ship.

---

## Header style

- **H1:** Page title. Declarative. Names the jurisdiction and topic. Example: "Statutory employer obligations under Indian law: EPF, ESI, gratuity, and professional tax."
- **H2:** Section heading. Sentence case. Often a statute name or topic. Example: "Employees' Provident Fund."
- **H3:** Sub-section. Often a section reference or sub-topic. Example: "§ 6 — Employer contribution rate."
- Avoid: listicle headers ("The 5 Things You Need to Know"), question headers ("What is EPF?" is acceptable only if followed by a strict definition, not advice).

---

## Forbidden words and phrases

Block these outright. The QA sub-agent flags any occurrence:

- "you should," "you must," "you'll need," "you can," "you may want to"
- "we recommend," "we suggest," "our advice"
- "save," "savings," "cost-effective," "affordable" (commercial framing)
- "transform," "skyrocket," "supercharge," "unlock," "leverage" (as verb)
- "best-in-class," "leading provider," "industry-leading"
- "tap into," "harness," "deep dive," "let's explore"
- "in today's globalized economy," "in our increasingly remote world"
- "amazing," "incredible," "revolutionary," "game-changing"
- "compliance headache," "red tape"
- "consult a lawyer" (the publication does not direct readers — that's advisory; the legal-advice disclaimer in the footer handles this)

---

## Required words and phrases (use freely)

- under, pursuant to, codified at, as enacted, as amended, in force, repealed
- provides, requires, prohibits, mandates, permits, establishes
- statute, regulation, decree, gazette, ministry, department, bureau
- jurisdiction, applicable law, governing law, foreign employer, domiciled
- section, sub-section, article, clause, paragraph, schedule
- last updated, verified against, as of, accessed

## Joel's byline

- **Display:** "Edited by Joel Deutsch" — never "Written by," never "Authored by."
- **Tagline beneath byline:** "Editor, Offshore Hiring Laws."
- **Bio:** Three sentences. Establishes credibility for editorial role without stating or implying legal practice. Example:

  > "Joel Deutsch is the founder of F5 Hiring Solutions and has overseen the placement of remote professionals from India and the Philippines into US-based companies since 2017. He edits Offshore Hiring Laws as an independent publication. He is not licensed to practice law."

- **Linkable bio page:** `/about` includes a longer-form version (5–7 sentences) with sourced credentials, the F5 Wikidata `sameAs` reference, and the explicit non-lawyer disclosure.

## Tone in tables

- **Column headers:** Title case, neutral. Example: "Governing Statute," "Section," "Employer Rate," "Wage Base / Cap," "Notes."
- **Statute names:** Cormorant Garamond italic where called out inline; in tables, regular Source Serif Pro is fine.
- **Section references:** IBM Plex Mono, e.g., "§ 6" or "Art. 297."
- **Currency:** Native currency named in the column header.
- **Missing data:** "Not specified by statute" or "Subject to state notification" — never blank, never "N/A" alone without context.

---

## Tone in callouts and notes

Callouts are reserved for jurisdictional caveats, definitional clarifications, or cross-references to other statutes. Never for marketing or emphasis.

**Example acceptable callout (methodology note):**

> *Methodology note:* The contributions listed reflect the central statutory schedule. State governments in India may impose additional obligations (e.g., professional tax rates and labour welfare fund contributions) which vary by state. This page references the central law only; state-specific obligations are noted where applicable but not exhaustively catalogued.

**Example acceptable caveat (statutory limitation):**

> *Statutory limitation:* The 13th month pay benefit under Presidential Decree No. 851 applies to rank-and-file employees only. Managerial employees are excluded under § 2 of the Decree, as interpreted by DOLE Implementing Rules.

---

## Treatment of statutory excerpts

When a statute is quoted directly, use a blockquote with proper attribution above and below:

> *§ 6, Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952 (India):*
>
> > The contribution which shall be paid by the employer to the Fund shall be ten percent of the basic wages, dearness allowance and retaining allowance, if any, for the time being payable to each of the employees…
>
> *Codified at IndiaCode. [URL]. Accessed May 2026.*

Quoted material kept under 75 words per excerpt (general fair-use practice for statutory text in commentary publications, though statutes are typically not copyrightable; this is a self-imposed restraint).

---

## Length norms

- Cornerstone pages: 2,500–4,500 words. Methodology page: 2,000–3,500.
- Paragraphs: 2–4 sentences. Statute-citing paragraphs may run longer when the citation requires full context.
- Sentences: median 20–28 words. Longer than Cost Index by design — legal-reference prose tolerates longer sentences when they carry full citation context.

---

## What good looks like

A reader who lands on this site should describe it as: *"It's an independent reference publication that aggregates the actual statutes and regulations governing offshore employment in India and the Philippines. It cites the primary sources, names the jurisdiction in every paragraph, and explicitly states it's not legal advice. The editor is Joel Deutsch, who runs a remote workforce company called F5 Hiring Solutions but is not a lawyer."*

A reader should never describe this site as: *"It's a guide to hiring offshore — it tells you what to do."*

If the second description fits at any point, the page fails Gate 4 (Voice) and Gate 5 (Legal-reference framing) in OHL-CONTENT-QA.md.
